Residential Decarbonization

Redidential Decarbonization is essential for reducing the carbon emissions associated with residential buildings. This can overall help one reduce the cost of energy bills, improve indoor air quality, and increase the comfort.



Facts

    • "nearly one-fifth of the nation’s carbon dioxide emissions come from the residential sector" - jchs.harvard.edu

      Stand by power accounts for about ~5-10% of residential energy use, and could cost the average U.S. household as much as $100 per year. - energy.gov

      "Always on" devices such as computers and TVs account for 3 percent of power consumption in the average represent roughly a quarter of your electricity bill. - NRDC.got

    Kill A Watt Usafe Monitor is a device that can be used to monitor the amount of energy your "always on" devices are using, for you to decide weather it is senseable to keep them plugged in or not..
